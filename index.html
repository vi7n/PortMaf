About

I’m a robotics & autonomous‑systems engineer finishing an M.S. in Robotics (minor in AI) at Oregon State University (graduating March 2025). My sweet‑spot lies where robotics hardware meets machine‑learning‑driven decision making. Over the last five years I’ve designed perception and control stacks for underwater manipulators, electric motorcycles, and 1⁄10‑scale autonomous racers—always with an eye toward robust deployment in messy, real‑world conditions.

My technical toolkit ranges from deep learning for 6‑DOF pose prediction (CNNs, spatiotemporal transformers) to probabilistic motion planning & SLAM (D* Lite, EKF/UKF, graph‑based optimizers) and real‑time middleware such as ROS 2. I’m equally at home profiling CUDA kernels on an NVIDIA Jetson or tuning MPC controllers in a Gazebo sim. Recent highlights include:

  • 70 % grasp success in a wave‑tank with dynamic underwater targets—leveraging a CNN‑based wave‑motion predictor.
  • 40 % localization‑drift reduction for Yatri Motorcycles through vision–inertial fusion and a Mapbox‑based simulator.
  • Sub‑20 ms end‑to‑end inference on embedded GPUs via TensorRT & model quantization.

Beyond the code, I mentor junior engineers, contribute to open‑source (ROS 2 perception and RDML tools), and enjoy translating cutting‑edge research into products that make autonomy trustworthy. If you’re looking to push the frontier of intelligent machines—let’s talk!
